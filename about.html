About

I have spent thirty-two years at the bedsides of strangers.

I became a registered nurse because I wanted to help people in their hardest moments. I spent the next three decades doing exactly that — across emergency rooms, intensive care units, and the long aftermath of lives changed in an instant.

I learned the language of medicine. I learned how a body breaks, and how it heals. I learned what to say to a family when there are no good words left.

And I learned something the medical system does not say out loud: the patients who do best are the ones who are not alone. The ones whose families understand what is happening. The ones who have someone steady at the bedside translating, documenting, and holding the room together.

Most people do not have that person.

The Clinical Doula is the person I wish every family had — the one I tried to be for every patient I cared for, and the one I now offer formally, fully, and with the time to truly show up.

I am also a mother and a grandmother. I have sat at the bedsides of the people I love and watched the system fail them, despite the brilliance and care of the people working inside it. The work I do now is shaped by what I learned in those rooms — and by what I wish someone had done for me.
